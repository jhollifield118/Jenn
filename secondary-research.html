Secondary Research

Secondary research consits of seeking out other sites for helpful solutions and information.

Researching cancer institutes and centers such as the Mayo Clinic and AnMed Health, can help gather ideas and information about treatment options.

There are many different types of cancer and each require a different method of treatment. A few examples of cancers are brain cancer, lymphoma, melanoma, and leukemia, etc.

According to the American Cancer Society, 1.8 million people a year are diagnosed with cancer and approximately 600,000 die from cancer per year. There are many possible treatments of cancer such as chemotherapy, surgery, radiation, immunotherapy, targeted therapy, hormone therapy, and stem cell transplants. Immunotherapy is a type of biological therapy that helps the immune system fight cancer.

Cancer may develop in anyone at any time, but historically, cancer is diagnosed in men more often than women. The National Cancer Institute estimates that one in two men and one in three women will be diagnosed with cancer during their lifetime. Also, men are less likely to survive cancer.

What treatment has had the highest success rate for patients? Ibrance, a targeted biologic therapy used for chemotherapy, has been developed to treat breast cancer - the most common type of cancer. The drug has been approved to use on advanced and secondary cases of breast cancer, and is administered in combination with a hormone therapy

The most successful hospital in the USA for cancer as of today is the University of Texas MD Anderson Cancer Center in Houston, Texas.

First, I recognized what the three main goals I believe Slow Food Upstate communicated. I did not establish these goals so that I could match them to my audience, but they served as more of loose guidlines and overarching categories.

After determining those broad goals, I made categories: Audience (as an Organization), Audience (Consumers/Earth Market), Sponsors, Partners, and Skeptics. I then proceded to fit a wide range and variety of audiences into each category.

I soon found that there were some audiences that fit into multiple categories. I wanted to cover all my bases, so I wedged them between columns to show interchangability. It was hard narrowing them down even further and deciding which section of the determined audience would fit best.

When it came down to refining my audience in this exercise, I focused on the categories Audience (as an Organization) and Audience (Consumers/Earth Market). I found that these two categories directly represented the main audience I wanted to reach.
